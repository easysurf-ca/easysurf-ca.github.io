Meet our team

We're your complete accessibility team—providing expert insights grounded in real-life experiences.

Who we are

Where expertise meets empathy—that's Easy Surf.

We're a collective of accessibility experts, each drawing from our in-depth knowledge and unique perspectives to offer you both comprehensive and genuine insights.

Our team includes a certified Accessibility Specialist (CPACC) and a community of Accessibility Consultants who identify as having a disability (or many). All our Consultants are everyday users of accessible technologies, and their lived experiences enrich our team's ability to identify accessibility issues and provide genuinely inclusive solutions that welcome everyone. They're also Content Creators who actively share and participate within the accessibility community through their various social media platforms.

Socials

Laetitia Bonaldo (CPACC)

Laetitia Bonaldo is the creator of Easy Surf and an Accessibility Specialist with a CPACC certification with the IAAP.

She currently provides auditing and consultation services to MS Canada and local BC businesses. She worked for 7 years in the technology industry, focusing on technical writing, product content, and localization.

She has extensive experience working with Developers, Designers, User Experience Researchers, Quality Assurance Testers, and Product Managers.

Noah Senecal-Junkeer

Noah Senecal-Junkeer is a Consultant and Accessibility Tester. Due to Complex Regional Pain Syndrome (CRPS), he uses speech recognition software and a headmouse while using his computer or mobile device.

He's experienced in providing accessibility feedback to Designers and Developers, including those from Fortune 500 companies. He has eight years of experience using Voice Control, Dragon NaturallySpeaking and Voice Access.

Noah has a BCom from McGill University and a background in investment banking, working as a Research Analyst at Scotiabank and Jarislowky Fraser.

Tamara Cable

Tamara Cable is an Accessibility Consultant and has over 15 years of experience as a consultant, lecturer, and developer.

Legally blind since birth and having lost most of her remaining vision in her early teens, she has been a lifelong user of assistive technology, a Braille reader, and a tech enthusiast. Additionally, she lives with other physical disabilities, which necessitate the use of a wheelchair and other mobility aids.

She works with a diverse range of clients, from mom-and-pop businesses to educational institutions and Fortune 500 companies.

Her extensive experience encompasses screen reader testing/accessibility (VoiceOver (iOS/Mac), NVDA, JAWS, TalkBack), website and application/game testing and development, brick-and-mortar accessibility and design, inclusivity lecturing, and more.

Victor Dima

Victor Dima, a blind gaming, technology, and entertainment journalist and Accessibility Consultant, resides in Oslo with his wife, Alina. Renowned internationally, he collaborates with Nordic PR agencies, publishers, and developers as an ambassador for universal design and accessibility. Victor has partnerships with Mi5 Communications and Xbox Nordic, receiving an Xbox Series X bundle and other gaming equipment.

Since 2010, Victor has been a prominent figure in gaming journalism, receiving early access to devices like PSVR2 and PS5 from Sony and frequently reviewing games and hardware before their release. His articles and reviews have achieved significant reach and engagement on social media platforms. He also runs a YouTube channel with over 1.5k subscribers.

Victor's expertise and connections within the disabled community make him a valuable voice in the industry. Here are a few of his stats:

  • His articles reach tens to hundreds of thousands of gamers.
  • His God of War Ragnarok review on LinkedIn received 50,000 impressions and 500+ reactions.
  • His The Last of Us Part I review reached over 30,000 people on LinkedIn, while reviews for Hogwarts Legacy and Xbox Series X gained over 10,000 impressions.
  • His Diablo IV review had over 20,000 reach on Twitter.
  • His reviews for Forza Motorsport and Mortal Kombat 1 each achieved over 20,000 impressions on LinkedIn in the first week.
  • His review for As Dusk Falls received over 12,000 views on Twitter.

Juan Alcazar

Juan Alcazar is a legally blind Accessibility Consultant, filmmaker, and content creator.

In 2017, he began his disability journey on YouTube after openly discussing his sight loss on his channel. He's now an advocate for accessibility and hopes to spread the word about disability and blindness through the medium of film and video.
