Connect your website to the entire world with accessible and effortless online experiences. Surfing the web should be easy for everyone.

Trust a partner with lived experience

Our collective is not just diverse in terms of expertise, nationality, and identity. It also includes a wide range of perspectives from people who identify as disabled.

We have a deep knowledge of assistive technology, which allows us to share our in-depth understanding of the end user's experiences.

We also acknowledge the unmet needs of the neurodiverse and neurodevelopmental disability communities.

Achieve more than just conformance

Let our community of experts make sure your website is WCAG conformant and welcoming to people with disabilities, for the benefit of your entire audience. Accessibility is usability.

Prevent tomorrow’s problems

Our shift left approach addresses accessibility issues early in the design phase. It prevents issues, reduces costs, requires less effort, and saves you time.

Support that stokes progress

Not sure where to start? We can do an intro audit for 1-3 pages of your website. Our Accessibility Specialist will identify high-impact accessibility issues and priority fixes. This puts you on the path to WCAG conformance, as conducting a full audit afterward would build upon the intro audit.

Mini audits are best paired with accessibility testing and interviews.

Full audits

Have experience implementing accessibility issues, and want to become WCAG conformant? We can do a full audit of your website. We’ll audit a representative sample of your website and key flows against the WCAG success criteria relevant to your accessibility goals.

We like to combine our full audits with accessibility testing and interviews. This approach captures the experience of everyday users of adaptive technology to support our audit findings.

Accessibility Testing and Interviews

Accessibility testing and interviews are recorded sessions with Accessibility consultants tailored to their specific needs. These sessions cover both desktop and mobile experiences, using various assistive technologies to ensure we capture diverse perspectives.

We recommend a minimum of 3 participants with various perspectives (ie. screen reader, magnification, and alternative navigation).
